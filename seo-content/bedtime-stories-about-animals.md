## Why Kids Love Animal Bedtime Stories

### Animals Feel Safe
Unlike human characters, animals exist in a slightly magical space. A bunny in a story doesn't have school stress or sibling rivalry ‚Äî she just wants to find a cozy burrow and curl up. That simplicity is soothing.

### They're Universal  
Whether your child is 2 or 7, a story about a sleepy owl works. Animals transcend the specific ages and experiences that can make "kid" characters feel too old or too young.

### Soft and Cuddly Imagery
Furry creatures, warm nests, fluffy tails ‚Äî animal stories naturally evoke the soft, cozy feelings perfect for bedtime.

### Gentle Lessons
Animals can model good bedtime behavior without being preachy. A bear who yawns and stretches before finding his cave teaches through example.

## Animals and Sleep: The Connection

Many of the animals in our stories naturally connect to sleep and nighttime:

- **Owls** ‚Äî creatures of the night, wise and watchful
- **Bears** ‚Äî hibernate, know how to settle in for a long rest
- **Bunnies** ‚Äî burrow into cozy underground homes
- **Mice** ‚Äî small and quiet, nest in soft materials
- **Squirrels** ‚Äî gather and prepare, then rest

These animals model what we want our kids to do: prepare for sleep, find a cozy spot, and peacefully drift off.
