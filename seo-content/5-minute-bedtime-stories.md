## Why Short Bedtime Stories Work

Research shows that bedtime routines help children fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly. But here's the thing: **the story doesn't need to be long to be effective**. What matters is:

- **Consistency** — reading together every night
- **Calm tone** — keeping your voice soft and steady  
- **Cozy setting** — dim lights, comfortable space
- **Gentle endings** — stories that end with sleep, safety, or home

A well-crafted 5 minute story can do all of this just as well as a 20 minute epic.

## Tips for Reading 5 Minute Stories

Even a short story becomes magical with the right delivery:

1. **Slow down** — Read slightly slower than you think you should
2. **Lower your voice** — Especially toward the end
3. **Pause at pictures** — Let them look, don't rush
4. **End with a ritual** — "And they all slept soundly until morning" + goodnight kiss
5. **Keep lights dim** — Avoid bright screens before this moment

## When You Need Even Shorter

Some nights, 5 minutes feels like 5 too many. We get it. Try our **2 minute micro-stories** — tiny tales that are basically extended goodnight wishes with a bit of story magic sprinkled in.

Or simply describe what your child did today as if it were a story: *"Once upon a time, there was a little girl named Sophie who went to the park and found a beautiful red leaf..."*
